Video Game Designers

Job Description

Video game designers develop user-friendly games for computers, mobile devices, and websites by brainstorming game details, building code, and testing products for efficiency. These individuals may create plots for games and organize ideas on flowcharts. Candidates must also solve problems that surface in early game drafts for usability and professional quality.
Designers concentrate on certain factors of game design, such as setting, and may work with other gaming professionals to build or test games. Game design candidates can perform these tasks for gaming companies like Nintendo, Ubisoft, and Gameloft. Graduates commonly find work as software creators, web developers, and graphic designers for technology companies.

Required Skills

Many careers related to video game design require each candidate to obtain at least a bachelor's degree in a related field, such as computer science, computer graphics, software engineering, or game design. An exception to this rule involves web developer positions that may only call for an associate degree, although specialized positions in web development may require a bachelor's degree.
Companies may prefer candidates with a relevant master's degree since these advanced degrees verify field knowledge beyond the bachelor's level. Specifically, master's programs may explore player experience, biometrics, and business aspects of video game design more than undergraduate options.

Salary

Graphic designers in advertising, public relations, and related services make more than $10,000 more per year than graphic designers in the printing and related support activities industry.
Salary expectations for video game designers vary with experience, with candidates in their first year averaging a $50,276 salary and designers with a minimum of 20 years of experience averaging $91,270.
Your specific industry impacts on your salary. For instance, the median pay for multimedia artists and animators who work for software publishers exceeds $80,000, while candidates in the advertising, public relations, and related services industry can earn a median of $67,330. Graphic designers in advertising, public relations, and related services make more than $10,000 more per year than graphic designers in the printing and related support activities industry.
Your location may impact earnings, as well. As an example, web developers earn more in Washington than in any other state, with Washington's average pay exceeding the national average by more than $18,000. Higher degrees and certifications can increase pay, too. World Education Services reports that candidates in visual arts average $1.966 million in a lifetime with a bachelor's. Master's degree holders average $2.3 million, and doctorate-holders average $2.5 million.
